CIVIC ISSUE GUIDELINES – PAKISTAN

Waste Management and Sanitation:
Improper waste disposal and accumulation of garbage is a common civic issue in many urban and rural areas of Pakistan. Citizens are encouraged to report such issues to their local municipal authorities. In major cities, this includes organizations such as Lahore Waste Management Company (LWMC), Sindh Solid Waste Management Board (SSWMB), and Capital Development Authority (CDA) in Islamabad.

If you encounter excessive garbage in your area, you should:
- Contact your local municipal office or waste management authority.
- Use official helplines or mobile applications (where available) to lodge a complaint.
- Provide accurate location details and, if possible, photographic evidence.
- Follow up on your complaint if the issue is not resolved within a reasonable time.

Maintaining cleanliness is a shared responsibility, and citizens are encouraged to avoid littering and to participate in community cleanliness initiatives.

Water Supply Issues:
Water shortages, leakages, and contamination are serious concerns in many parts of Pakistan. Water-related issues should be reported to the local water and sanitation agencies such as WASA (Water and Sanitation Agency) or relevant municipal departments.

Steps to address water issues:
- Report leaks, pipe bursts, or supply disruptions to WASA or the local authority.
- Avoid wastage of water and ensure proper storage practices.
- In case of contaminated water, inform authorities immediately and avoid consumption until resolved.
- Use filtration or boiling methods as a temporary safety measure.

Timely reporting helps prevent further damage and ensures quicker resolution.

Electricity Problems:
Electricity outages, load shedding, and faulty connections are commonly experienced issues. These should be reported to the respective electricity distribution companies such as LESCO, K-Electric, IESCO, or FESCO depending on your region.

Recommended actions:
- Contact the official complaint number of your electricity provider.
- Use online portals or SMS services for registering complaints.
- Check scheduled load shedding timings if applicable.
- Avoid handling electrical equipment or wires yourself, as this can be dangerous.

Providing meter number and location details can speed up the complaint process.

Gas Supply Issues:
Gas shortages and low pressure problems are often reported, especially during winter. These issues fall under the jurisdiction of Sui Northern Gas Pipelines Limited (SNGPL) and Sui Southern Gas Company (SSGC).

To handle gas-related issues:
- Report gas leakage immediately through emergency helplines.
- Do not attempt to fix gas pipelines on your own.
- Ensure proper ventilation in your home.
- Register complaints for low pressure or supply disruptions.

Safety should always be the top priority in gas-related matters.

Road and Infrastructure Issues:
Damaged roads, potholes, and broken street infrastructure can lead to accidents and inconvenience. These issues should be reported to local municipal corporations or development authorities.

Suggested steps:
- Identify the exact location of the issue.
- File a complaint through the relevant authority’s office or website.
- Engage with local representatives if necessary.
- Avoid using damaged roads when possible for safety reasons.

Proper infrastructure maintenance is essential for public safety and smooth transportation.

Crime and Public Safety:
In case of theft, robbery, harassment, or any criminal activity, immediate action should be taken. The primary authority responsible is the Police Department.

Steps to follow:
- Call the police emergency helpline (15) immediately.
- Visit the nearest police station to file a First Information Report (FIR).
- Provide accurate details of the incident.
- Avoid confronting suspects directly.

Citizens can also use digital platforms such as Police Khidmat Markaz or online complaint systems where available.

Public safety is a priority, and timely reporting helps law enforcement take appropriate action.

Cyber Crime:
Cyber crimes such as online fraud, hacking, and harassment are increasing concerns. These should be reported to the Federal Investigation Agency (FIA) Cyber Crime Wing.

Actions to take:
- Report incidents through the FIA cyber crime portal.
- Preserve evidence such as screenshots or transaction records.
- Avoid sharing sensitive personal information online.
- Use strong passwords and security measures.

Early reporting increases the chances of resolving such cases.

Emergency Services:
For emergencies such as fire, medical issues, or accidents, citizens should contact the relevant emergency services immediately.

Important helplines:
- Police: 15
- Ambulance (Edhi): 115
- Rescue Services (Rescue 1122): 1122
- Fire Brigade: Local city numbers

Quick response can save lives, so it is important to act without delay.

Conclusion:
Civic responsibility is shared between the government and citizens. While authorities are responsible for providing services, citizens must actively report issues and cooperate with officials. Proper use of complaint systems, awareness of relevant authorities, and responsible behavior can significantly improve the quality of life in communities across Pakistan.

This document serves as a general guideline to help individuals understand how to respond to common civic issues effectively and responsibly.
